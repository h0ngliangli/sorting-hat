As the BRSSD school district open survey for 2026-2027 middle school selection, 
I'm going to create a Sorting Hat-themed interactive guide to help students choose their middle school. 
This is a fun game where students will answer a series of questions and based on their answers, they will
be sorted into one of the middle schools in the district amaong the following options:
1. Sandpiper Middle School
2. Nesbit Middle School
3. Ralston Middle School
The game is purely for entertainment and engagement purposes, and it will not have any impact on the actual school selection process.
The sorting-hat is from the Harry Potter series, where a magical hat sorts students into different houses based on their personalities and preferences.
Below are the scene scripts for the interactive guide:

## Scene 1: Introduction
- The scene opens with a whimsical animation of the Sorting Hat appearing on the screen.
- The Sorting Hat introduces itself and explains the purpose of the game: "Welcome, young wizards and witches! I am the Sorting Hat, and I am here to help you discover which middle school in the BRSSD district is the best fit for you. Answer my questions honestly, and I will sort you into the school that matches your personality and preferences. Let's get started!"
- The scene ends with a prompt for the student to click "Start" to begin the sorting process.
## Scene 2: Question 1 - Learning Style
- The Sorting Hat asks the first question: "How do you prefer to learn?"
- The student is given three options to choose from:
  1. "I love hands-on activities and experiments."
  2. "I enjoy reading and writing."
  3. "I like to listen and discuss ideas with others."
- The student selects their answer and clicks "Next" to proceed to the next question.
## Scene 3: Question 2 - Extracurricular Interests
- The Sorting Hat asks the second question: "What extracurricular activities are you interested in?"
- The student is given three options to choose from:
  1. "I want to join sports teams and be active."
  2. "I am interested in clubs like drama, music, or art."
  3. "I want to participate in academic clubs and competitions."
- The student selects their answer and clicks "Next" to proceed to the next question.
## Scene 4: Question 3 - Social Preferences
- The Sorting Hat asks the third question: "What kind of social environment do you prefer?"
- The student is given three options to choose from:    
  1. "I like being in a large, bustling school with lots of students."
  2. "I prefer a smaller school where I can get to know everyone."
  3. "I want a school that has a good balance of social activities and quiet spaces."
- The student selects their answer and clicks "Next" to proceed to the sorting results.
## Scene 5: Sorting Results
- The Sorting Hat analyzes the student's answers and determines which middle school is the best fit for them based on their learning style, extracurricular interests, and social preferences.
- The Sorting Hat announces the result: "Based on your answers, I have sorted you into [School Name]! This school is a great fit for you because [reason based on answers]. Congratulations!"
- The scene ends with a prompt for the student to click "Finish" to end the game, and a message encouraging them to explore the actual school options in the BRSSD district for more information.
